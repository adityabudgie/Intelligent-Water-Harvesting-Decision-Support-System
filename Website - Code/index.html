01 — Abstract

A Region-Specific Lens on Water Scarcity

Water scarcity is not a uniform problem — its causes and possible solutions vary across different regions. In some areas, limited rainfall leads to shortages, while in others, resources such as rainwater or atmospheric moisture remain underutilized.

This project focuses on identifying region-specific water challenges and exploring suitable solutions instead of relying on a single method. To understand real-world needs, a survey and informal discussions were conducted with local residents.

The findings revealed that a large number of people face irregular water supply and depend on seasonal sources. Awareness regarding alternative water harvesting methods is relatively low — highlighting a clear gap between available solutions and their practical implementation.

Based on these observations, the project proposes an AI-based system that analyzes regional factors and user inputs to recommend the most suitable water management techniques, such as fog water harvesting or rainwater harvesting.

"Water scarcity is not due to lack of resources alone, but due to the mismatch between regional conditions and the solutions applied."

02–03 — Problem Statement

Understanding the
Water Crisis

The Core Problem

Water shortages are no longer limited to drought-prone zones. They are becoming prevalent in areas with high atmospheric moisture — like hilly regions and northern India where thick fog forms in winter. Principles like condensation, radiative cooling, and micro-droplet coalescence make it possible to collect water from fog and dew, yet environmental variations affect volume and purity.


There is a gap in developing systems that include real-time sensing and analysis. The inability to gather data on humidity, TDS, temperature, and air quality limits the ability to optimize these technologies and apply them broadly.

Observations from Survey

  • Majority of respondents reported irregular water supply
  • High dependence on groundwater and seasonal sources
  • Limited awareness of alternative water harvesting methods
  • Lack of solutions tailored to local conditions

Gaps Identified

The findings suggest the issue is not only water scarcity, but also the absence of region-specific solutions that match local requirements — calling for a more adaptive, flexible, and need-based approach.


Contributing Factors (Survey)

  • Agricultural overuse — tube wells, paddy irrigation (31.1%)
  • Climate change and rainfall variability (29.5%)
  • Population growth and urban demand (27.9%)
  • Poor water management policies
04–06 — Research & Field Work

What We Found on the Ground

A survey was conducted to understand how students perceive water scarcity, and field visits were made to farming areas to speak directly with farmers facing these challenges daily.

Student Awareness

Most students are quite aware of water scarcity as a growing problem affecting both urban and rural areas. 41% rated groundwater depletion in Punjab as a "very serious (major crisis)" issue.

Technology Optimism

67.2% of students rated AI-based water monitoring as "highly" or "moderately" effective in reducing water wastage. Many proposed combining IoT sensors and data analytics with weather forecasting.

Farmer Field Visit

Team members visited local farming areas and spoke directly with farmers. They shared struggles with irregular water supply and heavy dependence on rainfall, which has become increasingly unpredictable.

08–10 — Current Developments

Fog Harvesting Technology

In recent years, fog water harvesting has transitioned from rudimentary netting to highly engineered systems focused on maximizing collection efficiency, structural durability, and smart monitoring.

01 — Biomimetic Design

Janus Membranes

Inspired by the Namib Desert beetle and cactus spines, advanced meshes feature microscopic hydrophilic tips and hydrophobic valleys — replacing old polyolefin nets that captured only a small fraction of atmospheric moisture.

02 — Structural Innovation

3D Spacer Fabrics

The CloudFisher technology uses a robust three-dimensional mesh grid within a rigid frame — preventing violent flapping that previously shook off captured water. These structures withstand wind speeds up to 120 km/h.

03 — Smart Integration

IoT Monitoring

Current deployments incorporate IoT sensors providing real-time data on wind speed, humidity, and water yields. Operators can calculate efficiency, predict water availability, and detect mesh damage immediately.

04 — AI Decision Making

Rule-Based Intelligence

Our system implements a rule-based decision engine that mimics AI behavior: if humidity and soil moisture are low → suggest irrigation; if rainfall is high and tank is not full → suggest water harvesting.

20 — Constraints

Challenges Faced

Simulation Limitations

Many advanced sensors (humidity, turbidity, groundwater) are not directly available in Tinkercad. We used potentiometers to simulate sensor inputs, which depend on manual adjustment rather than detecting real-world conditions.

AI Implementation Constraints

Arduino and Tinkercad do not support full machine learning models. We implemented a rule-based decision engine — which demonstrates decision-making but cannot learn from data or adapt over time.

Circuit Design Issues

Incorrect wiring, improper grounding, and wrong component placement caused initial malfunctions. Debugging required careful observation, re-checking connections, and multiple testing iterations.

Time Constraints

Limited time prevented implementing advanced features such as real-time data collection, cloud integration, and advanced analytics and predictions — areas reserved for future development.

15 — Conclusion

Bridging the Gap Between
Potential and Practice

The survey and field interactions revealed that while there is a good level of awareness and innovative ideas among students, a gap remains between theoretical solutions and practical implementation. Bridging this gap requires collaboration between government bodies, researchers, engineers, and local communities — combined with cost-effective, region-specific solutions accessible across the country.
